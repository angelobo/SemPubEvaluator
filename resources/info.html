SemPub Challenge @ ESWC - Evaluation

This page describes the evaluation process to identify the best-performing approach of the Semantic Publishing Challenge at ESWC.

The result of a set of queries on the submitted LOD is compared with a gold standard, and precision and recall are measured. Both the gold standard and the output under evaluation are expected to be CSV files with a common structure. The evaluation is performed by a PHP software, available on GitHub, that compares each pair of corresponding CSV files.

Two evaluation modes are supported:

  • strict: only complete matches are considered correct, after some normalization
  • loose: more tolerant, some partial matches are considered correct too

For some queries these two modes overlap (same evaluation strategies).

General notes:

  • For each type of entry (es. article, funding agency, grant, ontology, etc.) specific comparison strategies are implemented (in the matchesEntry() method of the corresponding class).
  • The IRIs structure is ignored, as participants were free to use their own model and structure.
  • The order of the CSV entries is not relevant.
  • Unexpected duplicates are considered errors.

Also, the evaluation mechanisms are implemented so as to take minor differences into account and to normalize them:

  • All values are trimmed and transformed into lowercase.
  • Missing " symbols around CSV values are ignored.
  • Titles are normalized as follows: all spaces, punctuation symbols and non-ASCII characters are stripped, only letters and numbers are considered.
  • Names are normalized as follows: all spaces, punctuation symbols are stripped, non-ASCII characters are substituted with ASCII characters.
  • Missing datatypes are ignored.
