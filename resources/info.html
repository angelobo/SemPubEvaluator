SemPub Challenge @ ESWC 2015 - Evaluation

This page describes the evaluation process to identify the best-performing approach of the Semantic Publishing Challenge at ESWC 2015.

The result of a set of queries on the submitted LOD is compared with a gold standard, and precision and recall are measured. Both the gold standard and the output under evaluation are expected to be CSV files with a common structure. The evaluation is performed by a PHP software that compares each pair of corresponding CSV files.

Two evaluation modes are supported:

  • strict: only complete matches are considered correct (after some normalization)
  • loose: more tolerant, partial matches are considered correct too

General notes:

  • For each query only some properties are considered. For instance, the articles are compared by DOI and (normalized) titles only.
  • The IRIs structure is ignored, as participants were free to use their own model and structure.
  • According to the evaluation mode (strict or loose), the software verifies that all properties match or only some of them. Strict/loose matching rules are specific for each query.
  • The order of the CSV entries is not relevant.
  • Unexpected duplicates are considered errors.

Also, the evaluation mechanisms are implemented so as to take minor differences into account and to normalize them:

  • All values are trimmed and transformed into lowercase.
  • Missing " symbols around CSV values are ignored.
  • Titles are normalized as follows: all spaces, punctuation symbols and non-ASCII characters are stripped, only letters and numbers are considered.
  • Names are normalized as follows: all spaces, punctuation symbols are stripped, non-ASCII characters are substituted with ASCII characters.
  • Missing datatypes are ignored.
  • DOI information not available in the gold standard are skipped in the evaluation.
  • Ontologies are compared by their full names or their acronyms
